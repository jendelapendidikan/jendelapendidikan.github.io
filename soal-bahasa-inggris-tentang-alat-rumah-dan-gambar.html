9. Deskripsikan rumah adat joglo dengan bahasa inggris


Joglo is a type of traditional vernacular house of the Javanese people (Javanese omah). The word joglo refers to the shape of the roof. In the highly hierarchical Javanese culture, the type of the roof of a house reflects the social and economic status of the owners of the house; joglo houses is traditionally associated with Javanese aristocrats.

Joglo roof can be implemented to a dwelling (omah) or a pavilion (pendopo).

Jawaban panjang:

   Jambi is a province on the island of Sumatra. Jambi City was formed when the Jambi Malay Kingdom was founded around the XVII century. Located around the banks of the Batanghari River, Jambi was formed from various cultures originating from various ethnicities, social, economic strata, etc. which can be seen from the buildings and the atmosphere of the river banks.

   In the 70s, the Governor of Jambi once held a "Sepucuk Jambi Nine Houses" event to find a traditional house that became the identity of the Jambi area. From the results of the competition, the one chosen and used as the Jambi traditional house was the Kajang Leko Stage House.

  The Kajang Leko Stage House is an architectural concept from the Marga Bathin. Until now, the Bathin people still maintain the traditional customs that their predecessors responded to, even the Kajang Leko heritage can still be enjoyed its beauty and is still used today. One of the Batin villages that is still intact until now is Lamo Village in Rantau Panjang.

   The Kajang Leko Stage House has a rectangular shape with a size of approximately 12 meters x 9 meters. Its uniqueness lies in the construction structure & the carving art that adorns the building.

   

     The roof construction of the house is called the "drunken elephant" after the story of the name of the house builder who fell in love but was not approved. The ridge is made to curve onto the boat called "jerambah" or "fold kajang" with the top roof called kasau, made of woven fibers that are folded in half to prevent air from entering the house.

   On the ceiling of the house there is a divider called "screen spread" which serves to hold back the seepage of the rainwater. The space between the spread screen and the roof is usually used to store equipment. while on the edge, the walls of the house are made of wood on the edge with carvings.

   

     There are three kinds of doors to the kajang leko house, namely: upright doors, wall doors, and transverse turning doors.

     The Kajang Lako Stage House has 30 poles consisting of 24 main poles and 6 palamban poles.

     The Kajang Leko Stage House is a form of embodiment of the taste of art, culture and beliefs of the Jambi people, which are implied starting from the shape of the building, the function of the room, the art of carving, etc.

20. deskripsikan rumah adat joglo dalam bahasa inggris


Joglo is a traditional vernacular house of Javanese people. It consist of two parts; the pendopo and dalem. The pendopo is the front section of Joglo that have large roofed space with columns and without wall or partition. The dalem is the inner sections with walled enclosure and rooms such as bedroom and kitchen. Pendopo is used to receive guests, reception hall and living room, while the inner dalem are more private sections of the house. The term "Joglo" is often used to refer the distinctive type of Javanese roof with rising central part of roof supported by four or more main wooden columns (saka guru). The outer row of columns with rectangular plan created expansion spaces. The roof formed a pyramid-like structure with central part are taller and steeper. It is said that the roof of Joglo is constructed to mimic a mountain.

In a structured Javanese society and tradition, Joglo also reflect Javanese social status; this type of building is only reserved for palace (keraton), official residence, government estate, and the house of nobles (ningrat). Originally commoners are not allowed to construct this kind of house as their residence.
Every region in Indonesia has a form of house building that became a symbol, or characteristic of the area called the traditional house. Likewise with Java that has a variety of typical traditional house arsiktetkur scattered in every province of the traditional house Joglo. Traditional house Joglo Joglo there are nine types namely Limasan jokes, Joglo Sinom, Joglo Jompongan, Joglo Pangrawit, Joglo Mangkurat, Joglo Hageng, Joglo Semar Tinandhu, Holy Joglo, Joglo Jepara, and Joglo Pati.

23. deskripsikan rumah adat joglo dengan bahasa inggris


Joglo is a type of traditional Javanese house that looks simple and is used as a symbol or a marker of social status gentry or nobility of Java. This house has its own uniqueness or distinctiveness in the presence of pillars or pillar, along with his intercropping. Every part of the house represents a different function, which is built on a large area as well. Therefore, this house is only belongs to the people of the haves only.

Description of the rooms in the house joglo
Room at home joglo is generally divided into three main parts. The first part is called pendhopo meeting room. The second part is the living room or space that used to hold a puppet show, called pringgitan. The third part is called ndalem back room, and is used as a family room. In this space there are three senthong (room), namely senthong left, senthong middle, and right senthong.
