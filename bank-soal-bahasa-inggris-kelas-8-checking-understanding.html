12. Contoh percakapan checking understanding dalam bahasa inggris yang panjang untuk 4-5 orang


1. Checking understanding

There are some phrases can be used for checking understanding. For example:

- Do you understand?

- Do you know what I'm saying?

- Do you have any questions?

- Is it okay?

2. Asking and giving opinion

To ask other people's opinions, common phrase we can use is "What do you think of (something)?" of "How do you feel about (something)?"

While for giving opinion, we can start our sentence with "I think...".

3. Showing appreciation

Showing appreciation is also known as giving thanks or gratitude to someone or group of people who have helped us with something. Below are some sample phrases we can use to show appreciation.

- Thank you, I appreciate your help.

- Thank you.

- Thanks a lot.

- It was very kind of you.

- Thank you very much, it was very helpful.

Now let's try combining those phrases in a conversation.

Student A: Hey, have you watched Fantastic Beast?

Student B: Yeah, I watched it last month.

Student A: What do you think of the movie? Is it good?

Student B: I really like fantasy movie so I think it's very interesting.

Student A: I want to see it too but I don't know where can I watch it.

Student B: I can lend you the DVD if you're interested!

Student A: That would great. Thanks a lot!

Student B: I'll check if I can bring it to school tomorrow or not. If I can't, you might need to come to my house to get it. Is it okay?

Student A: Got it! Thanks again!

14. Buatlah dialog bahasa inggris yang isi percakapannya itu ada attention, checking for understanding, compliment, opinion, dan ability


Sebelum masuk ke jawaban pertanyaan ini, sebelumnya perlu dipahami tentang fungsi dan apa saja ekspresi yang digunakan untuk meminta perhatian (asking for attention), memberi pendapat (giving opinion), dan mengecek pemahaman (checking understanding).


Asking for Attention


Ungkapan ini digunakan untuk meminta perhatian orang lain atau lawan bicara, agar memperhatikan apa yang dikatakan. Beberapa ekspesi yang umumnya digunakan adalah:


* May I have your attention, please.


* Listen to me, please.


* Look, .......


* Listen carefully, ......


Giving Opinion


Giving opinion digunakan untuk mengawali perkataan yang isinya tentang menyampaikan pendapatnya pada seseorang atau lawan bicara. Beberapa contoh untuk ekspresi giving opinion adalah:


* I think you should......


* From my point of view ...


* As far as I am concerned ...


* I guess that ...


Checking understanding


Ekspresi ini digunakan untuk mengetahui apakah lawan bicara memahami perkataan kita. Contoh beberapa ekspresi yang seringkali digunakan adalah:


* Did you understand what I said?


* Do you know what I mean?


* Did you get the point?


* Are you with me?


Contoh dialog yang mencakup asking for attention, giving opinion, dan checking understanding:


Stephen: dr. Palmer, I need you to listen to me now! (dr. Palmer aku minta Anda mendengarkan saya sekarang) =>asking for attention


dr. Palmer: What can I do for you, Stephen? (apa yang bisa kulakukan untukmu, Stephen?)


Stephen: I have a meeting today at 3 pm. Unfortunately, I think I can't attend it because I have already had an appointment with my friend, Wong. (Aku ada pertemuan jam 3 sore ini. Sayangnya, sepertinya aku tidak bisa datang karena sudah ada janji sebelumnya dengan temanku, Wong).


dr. Palmer: I think you should set your priority. If the meeting is important, the best is to rearrange your appointment with Wong. (menurutku kamu harus menentukan prioritas. Jika rapatnya penting, sebaiknya atur ulang janjimu bertemu Wong) => giving suggestion


Stephen: I have had the appointment long time ago. I don't think I can just cancel it. (Aku sudah lama janjian dengannya. Kupikir tidak bisa dibatalkan begitu saja).


dr. Palmer: Oh, Come on. Please, listen to me more carefully. (Ayolah. Tolong dengarkan baik-baik apa yang kukatakan). => asking for attention


Stephen: Alright. Tell me what to do. (Baiklah, Beritahu apa yang harus aku lakukan)


dr. Palmer: The meeting is an important one, right? Did you get my point? (Rapatnya penting, kan? Kamu mengerti maksudku?) => Checking understanding


Stephen: Yes, everyone is invited. We have urgent matter to discuss. (Ya, semua orang diundang ke rapat itu. Ada hal urgen yang harus dibahas).


dr. Palmer: Then I guess you can rearrange your appointment with Wong. Maybe tomorrow or the day after tomorrow. (Kalau begitu, sepertinya kamu bisa mengatur ulang janjimu dengan Wong. Mungkin besok atau lusa) => giving opinion


Stephen: I'll rethink about it. (Akan kupikirkan lagi)


Demikian contoh dialog yang mengandung minta perhatian (asking for attention), memberikan pendapat (giving opinion), dan memeriksa pemahaman (checking understanding) beserta artinya. Semoga bisa bermanfaat. Untuk mempelajari beberapa contoh lainnya silakan cek tautan-tautan ini:

33. contoh materi dialog pendek terkait checking one's understanding smp kelas 8


When should we check understanding?We should check that learners understand:
-when we are introducing or revising new language (grammar or lexical items and functional expressions)
-when, during an activity, we realise that our learners haven’t understood something. This could be, for example, a word in a reading text
-when we are giving instructions for an activity-when we are correcting errors-when feeding back for comprehension of a listening or reading activity
How can we check understanding?
Our choice should depend on the type of language that we’re checking. Different techniques lend themselves to different types of language. Here are a few different ways with some examples:
1 Using synonyms and antonymsExample: hot“What’s the opposite of hot?”
2 Eliciting or giving definitions or examplesExample: software“What are some examples of software?”
3 Getting the students to mark language on timelinesVery useful for verb forms – have students (in pairs, small groups, or at the whiteboard) mark the verb forms on a timeline
4 Concept questionsExample: “You don’t have to do your homework”“Is it necessary to do your homework?” (no)“Can I decide?” (yes)“Can I do my homework if I want to?” (yes)
5 Asking for personal responsesExample: bear“What would you do if you saw a bear?”
6 Using pictures or realiaVery effective when you have planned which language items you’re going to be checking (you can bring pictures or realia with you or use what is in the classroom). More difficult for ad hoc checking.Example with realia: shoes“Show me your shoes”
7 Asking students to mimeUseful for action verbs
8 Discriminating between different formsExample: “I ate my dinner when you arrived” / “I was eating my dinner when you arrived”– What’s the difference? (Learners could answer with timelines)
9 Using translationIf a language item has a direct and unambiguous translation in the learners’ L1, translation can be a quick and effective way of checking understanding if you don’t want to interrupt the flow of an activity. This requires a monolingual class (every learner has the same L1) and the teacher to be confident with his or her translation.
Checking understanding of instructions:After giving instructions clearly and concisely, we can check understanding by:
-Modelling the activity with one student-Asking one pair of students to model the activity-Asking students to repeat the instructions back to you – if you have broken the instructions down into clear steps, this becomes easier: “Luisa, what is the first thing you are going to do?”… “Ok, Rafael, what are you going to do next?”…
How not to check understandingAsking the question “Do you understand” can lead to trouble. There are a couple of reasons for this:
Learners may be afraid or shy to admit that they don’t understand. Loss of face with peers or the teacher can be an issue.Learners may think they understand but don’t. False friends are one reason for this. For example, a French student may think “actually” translates as “actuellement”. (“Actuellement” in fact translates more like “at the moment”, “currently” or “nowadays”).
