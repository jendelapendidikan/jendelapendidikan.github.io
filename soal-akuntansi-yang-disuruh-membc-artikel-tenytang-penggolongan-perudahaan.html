3. artikel tentang akuntansi ​


Jawaban:

Penjelasan:Akuntansi adalah proses mencatat, mengklasifikasikan, dan menganalisis transaksi keuangan. Ini bertujuan untuk menyediakan informasi yang berguna bagi para pemakai laporan keuangan, seperti pemilik, kreditur, dan pemerintah.

Akuntansi dibagi menjadi dua jenis utama: akuntansi keuangan dan akuntansi manajemen. Akuntansi keuangan menyediakan informasi keuangan tentang perusahaan untuk pihak luar, seperti pemilik, kreditur, dan investor. Akuntansi manajemen, di sisi lain, menyediakan informasi kepada manajemen perusahaan untuk membantu mereka dalam mengambil keputusan bisnis.

Dalam akuntansi keuangan, ada beberapa laporan keuangan utama yang disusun berdasarkan standar akuntansi yang ditetapkan. Laporan keuangan utama ini meliputi neraca, laporan laba rugi, dan laporan arus kas. Neraca menunjukkan aset, liabilitas, dan ekuitas perusahaan pada suatu waktu tertentu. Laporan laba rugi menunjukkan pendapatan dan biaya perusahaan dalam periode waktu tertentu. Laporan arus kas menunjukkan aliran masuk dan keluar kas dari perusahaan dalam periode waktu tertentu.

Dalam akuntansi manajemen, ada beberapa alat yang bisa digunakan oleh manajemen untuk membantu dalam mengambil keputusan bisnis. Alat-alat ini meliputi anggaran, sistem pengendalian biaya, dan sistem pengukuran kinerja. Anggaran adalah suatu rencana keuangan yang menggambarkan harapan perusahaan tentang masa depan. Sistem pengendalian biaya digunakan untuk mengidentifikasi dan mengelola biaya-biaya yang timbul dalam suatu proses produksi. Sistem pengukuran kinerja menggunakan indikator-indikator kinerja untuk mengukur seberapa baik perusahaan mencapai tujuannya.

Akuntansi merupakan bagian yang penting dari setiap perusahaan, karena membantu dalam menyediakan informasi keuangan yang dibutuhkan oleh pihak-pihak yang berkepentingan. Selain itu, akuntansi juga membantu dalam pengambilan keputusan bisnis yang tepat oleh manajemen perusahaan.

4. Biografi bahasa inggris dan terjemahnya tenytang di sekolah


Isaac Newton (1642 - 1727) was one of the greatest scientists in history. Newton was born in 1642 in Woolsthorpe, Lincolnshire, England. When he was in school, he did not show his brilliant intelligence, but luckily his teacher encouraged him to continue his studies.


In 1661, Little Newton entered Trinity College in Cambridge. Four years later, he graduated and planned to continue his studies to obtain a master's degree. However, at that time there was an outbreak of bubonic plague, so the university closed. Newton returned to Woolsthorpe, and two years later he laid the foundations for contributing to physics, mathematics and astronomy.


For more than 20 years he was very productive and at the age of 45 he published his famous work: Philosophiae Naturalis Principia Mathematica (Mathematical Principle of Natural Philosophy), or commonly abbreviated as Principia. In this book he writes his laws about motion together with the theory of gravity. The publication of the Principia was funded by his friend Edmund Halley, who used Newton's theory to predict the return of Halley's comet.

Swiss Banks – A Safe Haven for Money from Around the World

Switzerland is not only well-known for chocolate and its watch-making industry. It is also famous for its unique banking and financial system. Banks play an important role in Switzerland’s economy. Swiss banks have been considered to be the most secretive in the world for many centuries. About a third of all money held outside a person’s home country – hundreds of billions of dollars – is deposited in Swiss banks.

Banking in Switzerland goes back to the 18th century. In the past, not only rich people but also authoritarian regimes and dictators have secured their money in Swiss banks. Foreigners who live in unstable and troubled areas also tend to keep money in secure Swiss banks instead of their own. During World War II German Nazis deposited much of the country’s wealth, especially money and gold taken away from Jews, in Switzerland.

Switzerland has two large banks – UBS and Credit Suisse. Private banking also has a century-long tradition in the alpine country. Currently, about 130000 people work in the banking sector. Major banks have overseas branches that employ thousands. Switzerland’s popularity as a safe bank haven has many reasons. For one, the country has a very stable and prosperous economy, and one of the highest per capita incomes in the world. It has been neutral for many centuries, especially during the two world wars. In addition, the Swiss franc is one of the most stable currencies in the world.

Swiss banks have to follow very strict privacy rules. In 1934, a law was passed that forbade Swiss banks to reveal the name of account holders. Banks protect the privacy of their clients in a way no other country in the world does. If a Swiss banker reveals anything about a person’s bank account without permission he commits a criminal act. Such regulations have made Switzerland a popular destination for foreigners who do not want to pay taxes in their own country.

Lately, however, the Swiss government has been under constant pressure by other countries to change their bank laws, making it possible to reveal the names of possible drug dealers or international criminals. EU officials claim that more and more citizens are moving their money to Switzerland to escape taxation in their home country. Income that is not reported to the government is often deposited in Swiss banks.

In the past decade, Swiss bank officials have been working together with international financial experts to combat money laundering. Under new laws, they and must report any financial transactions that look suspicious.
