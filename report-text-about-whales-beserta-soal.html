12. Perbedaan perbedaan whales dan fish


Whales (mammals) breathe air with lungs, provide milk to their young, give live birth, are warm-blooded, have hair, have eyelids and tear ducts, do not have scales, have arm bones, hand bones, and finger bones, do not possess bony fins, and evolved from other land-dwelling mammals.

Fish breathe water with gills, do not provide milk to their young, typically lay eggs (except for unusual cases in which the eggs hatch internally), are typically cold blooded (with a few fish being somewhere in between warm and cold-blooded), typically have scales, do not have hair, do not have eyelids or tear ducts, do not have arm bones, hand bones, or finger bones, typically have bony fins, and evolved from primitive aquatic pre-fish ancestors.
